# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and fellow developers scanning quickly between tabs. They arrive from LinkedIn, GitHub, or a resume link with 30–90 seconds of attention. They need to understand who Bhargesh is, what he builds end-to-end, and how to reach him without hunting.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that communicates engineering craft through the site itself. Success means a visitor leaves knowing Bhargesh is a software engineer who ships full web features — UI, APIs, and data — can point to real projects, and is easy to contact for contract work.

Profile copy and facts live in [`PROFILE.md`](PROFILE.md); update that file before changing site content.

## Brand Personality

Confident, precise, unhurried. Three words: **crafted**, **clear**, **quiet**. The site should feel like a well-edited introduction, not a dashboard or a playground.

## Anti-references

- Purple gradients, glassmorphism, frosted bento cards
- "Today's Joke" widgets, smiley easter eggs, novelty interactions
- Dense dashboard grids, side-stripe borders, gradient text
- Generic AI landing page tropes: "Boost your productivity", hero blobs, floating cards
- The previous portfolio's skull backgrounds, topography textures, and wobble animations

## Design Principles

1. **Show craft through restraint** — whitespace, type hierarchy, and alignment do the work; decoration does not.
2. **One clear path** — hero introduces, experience proves tenure, projects prove output, footer converts to contact.
3. **Practice what you preach** — the portfolio must load fast and read well on mobile; the layout and copy should reflect the same clarity expected in product work.
4. **Earn every pixel** — no section exists without a job; if it doesn't inform hire or contact, cut it.
5. **Accessible by default** — readable contrast, focus states, reduced-motion respect, semantic structure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA contrast for text and interactive elements. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Keyboard-navigable links and focus-visible outlines on all interactive elements. Alt text on images and icons.
